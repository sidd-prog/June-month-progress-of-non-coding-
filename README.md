Work Completed

This month, I focused on building a strong research foundation for our railway project. I completed the assigned data entry work and prepared the datasets and corresponding data schemas that will be used by the development team. Alongside this, I researched railway attributes, operations, and documented several research papers relevant to Indian Railways and railway technology.

As I progressed, I realized that understanding Indian Railways requires much more than simply reading documents. Every topic—whether delays, signalling, rake management, or station operations—is interconnected. Because of this, I spent a significant amount of time understanding how different systems interact rather than treating them as isolated subjects. Although this took longer than expected, it gave me a much clearer picture of the railway ecosystem.

One of my key contributions this month was identifying the areas our team still needs to research. I organized these into a structured roadmap so that our future work can be more systematic instead of approaching topics randomly.

---

Self-Evaluation

Hours Worked: 74 hours

Looking back, I achieved the objectives I had set for this month, particularly in research, documentation, and dataset preparation. However, I also feel that I could have converted more of the time I spent into tangible deliverables. A considerable portion of my work involved understanding unfamiliar railway concepts before I could document them properly. While that learning was necessary, I believe I can manage my time more efficiently going forward.

The biggest takeaway from this month is that a strong understanding of the railway domain is just as important as building the software itself. Rather than rushing into solutions, I now have a better appreciation of how railway operations function and where technology can realistically add value.

For the next month, my goal is to deepen my understanding of railway operations, produce higher-quality documentation, and contribute research that is directly useful to both the coding and non-coding teams. I also want to be more proactive in identifying problems and proposing practical, well-researched solutions instead of only documenting existing knowledge.
